    <note boxed>
        <h2>Power</h2>
        <p>Power is defined as the rate energy (<b>U</b>) is transformed or transferred over time. We measure power in units of joules/second, also known as watts.</p>
        <equation>
            \text{power} &= \frac{\mathrm{d}U}{\mathrm{d}t}
        </equation>
        <p>An electric circuit is capable of transferring power.</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Current is the rate of flow of charge</li>
            <li>voltage measures the energy transferred per unit of charge</li>
        </ul>
        <p>We can insert these definitions into the equation for power:</p>
        <equation>
            \text{power}
                &= \frac{\mathrm{d}U}{\mathrm{d}t}\\
                &= \frac{\mathrm{d}U}{\mathrm{d}q}\cdot\frac{\mathrm{d}q}{\mathrm{d}t}\\
                &= v \cdot i
        </equation>
        <p>Electrical power is the product of voltage times current. in units of watts.</p>
    </note>
